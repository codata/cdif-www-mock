A major goal of CDIF is to streamline the workflow from needing particular data to having it accessible to the software you're writing. We rely on the community for feedback, development, and implementation ideas.

### Get Involved

- **Join the Working Groups:** The CDIF Working Group meets virtually every two weeks. [Use this form to apply](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSfC6BoxSnAZOMWZ65pNTiljnsBXjfv80NgEeALPcY5DPaESHA/viewform).
- **Stay Updated:** [Register to join the CDIF community mailing list](https://bit.ly/cdif-community-list).
- **Contribute Code:** Project development is open source. Check out the [Cross-Domain Interoperability Framework on GitHub](https://github.com/Cross-Domain-Interoperability-Framework).

### To Learn More

- 📖 **The CDIF Book:**  First released in May 2024 as an output of the WorldFAIR project, the definitive point of reference for CDIF and its component profiles is now the [CDIF Book](https://book.cdif.org).
- 🤖 **[Chat with CDIF](https://notebooklm.google.com/notebook/5833376b-c169-433e-9743-0b6b3a928585):** Engage with an AI-driven exploration of the standards, guidelines, and documentation for CDIF.
- 🎥 **Webinar:** [Presentations and recording from our latest webinar](https://codata.org/the-cross-domain-interoperability-framework-cdif-practical-guidelines-for-fair-interoperability-webinar-25-july/).
