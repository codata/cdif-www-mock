# Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026

The Cross-Domain Interoperability Framework (CDIF) is a project supported by CODATA. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring you have a positive experience on our website.

## Information We Collect
We collect minimal and non-identifiable information to improve your website experience:
- **Usage Data**: We may collect non-personally identifiable information automatically via fundamental server logs. This information includes browser type, device type, requesting URLs, and timestamps.
- **Cookies**: We utilize strictly necessary local storage (such as managing your local theme and hero background preferences via Alpine.js) which remain on your device. We do not use third-party tracking or advertising cookies.

## How We Use Information
We use the information we collect solely for the following purposes:
- To present our website and its contents to you.
- To store your accessibility preferences, such as Dark Mode or Light Mode themes.
- To detect, prevent, and address technical issues safely and efficiently.

## Data Sharing and Disclosure
We **do not** sell, trade, or rent your personal identification information to others. Usage data and broad demographic information not linked to any personal identification information may be shared with our partners, trusted affiliates, and funding bodies for statistical purposes regarding framework adoption.

## External Links
Our Site contains links to other sites (such as GitHub or Zenodo). If you click on a third-party link, you will be directed to that site. Note that these external sites are not operated by us. We strongly advise you to review the Privacy Policy of these websites. We have no control over and assume no responsibility for the content, privacy policies, or practices of any third-party sites or services.

## Contact Us
If you have any questions or suggestions about our Privacy Policy, do not hesitate to contact us through our primary [GitHub Organization](https://github.com/Cross-Domain-Interoperability-Framework).
